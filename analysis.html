Metrics guide

Definitions for every KPI, chart, and insight on this page. Figures use the same P&L engine as Dashboard and Reports when buying prices are complete.

Getting started Period, data sources, and reconciliation

Select a range and click Run analysis. Results appear under Metrics, Charts, and Insights.

Data sources
Meter Reading (DSR), Expenses, billing invoices (lube), and receipt buying rates from Dashboard P&L.
Fuel litres
Net litres only — meter sales minus testing litres.
Buying price
Pre-VAT ₹/KL on every receipt day in range. Without it, net profit and related KPIs show “—”.
Reconciliation
Period gross profit and net profit match Dashboard P&L and Reports P&L for the same dates. Differs from Trading account gross income (stock-based).
Key metrics Primary financial KPIs
Total sales Fuel net sale + lube/billing

Sum of fuel net sale ₹ (net litres × selling rate) plus lube/billing invoice totals in the period.

Total expenses Operating expenses only

All logged expenses in the period. MS/HS testing and density test categories are excluded (tracked in day closing).

Gross profit Fuel margin + lube/billing
Formula Fuel gross profit = net litres × (selling rate − landed buying rate)
Gross profit = fuel gross profit + lube/billing sales

Profit before operating expenses. Shown under Profitability & costs on the Metrics tab. Blocked (“—”) until buying prices exist on all receipt days in range.

Net profit Gross profit − operating expenses
Formula Net profit = gross profit − total expenses

Blocked (“—”) until buying prices exist on all receipt days in range.

Sales growth vs previous period

Percentage change in total sales compared to the immediately preceding period of the same number of calendar days.

Profitability & costs Margins and per-litre economics
Fuel purchase costCost of fuel sold

Net litres sold × landed buying rate (pre-VAT ₹/KL + delivery + VAT/LST per litre) for each sale day.

Gross profitBefore operating expenses

Fuel gross profit (net litres × (selling − buying)) plus lube/billing sales (treated as full margin).

Gross marginGross profit ÷ total sales

Percentage of total sales retained as gross profit before expenses. Requires complete buying prices.

Cost ratioFuel cost ÷ total sales

Share of sales consumed by fuel purchase cost. Lower is better if selling rates are stable.

Avg. daily profitNet profit ÷ calendar days

Period net profit divided by all calendar days in the range (including days with no sales).

Avg. daily expensesExpenses ÷ calendar days

Total operating expenses divided by calendar days in the range.

Selling price per litre (MS / HSD)Volume-weighted meter rate

Average selling rate per litre for petrol (MS) or diesel (HSD): net sale ₹ ÷ net litres for that product in the period.

Buying price per litre (MS / HSD)Landed cost incl. VAT + delivery

Volume-weighted landed buying rate per litre for each product (pre-VAT ₹/KL + delivery + VAT/LST applied per sale day). Requires complete buying prices.

Gross margin per litreGross profit ÷ litres

Gross profit (fuel margin + lube/billing) spread across total petrol + diesel litres sold, before operating expenses. Requires complete buying prices.

Petrol / diesel gross margin per litreFuel gross profit ÷ product litres

Per-product fuel gross margin: net litres × (selling − landed buying incl. VAT + delivery), divided by petrol or diesel litres sold. Lube/billing is excluded from these product-specific figures.

Profit per litreNet profit ÷ litres

Net profit spread across total petrol + diesel litres sold. Requires complete buying prices.

Fuel volume & mix Litres and product split
Total volumePetrol + diesel (L)

Sum of net litres sold for petrol and diesel across the period.

Avg. daily volumeLitres per calendar day

Total volume divided by calendar days in the range.

Petrol / Diesel volumeNet litres by product

Net litres sold per fuel type (meter minus testing).

Petrol / Diesel share% of fuel revenue

Each product’s share of fuel revenue (₹), not volume. Petrol share + diesel share = 100%.

Performance & activity Trends, extremes, and trading days
Avg. daily salesTotal sales ÷ calendar days

Mean daily sales ₹ across the full selected range.

Profit marginNet profit ÷ total sales

Net profit as a percentage of total sales. Requires complete buying prices.

Expense ratioExpenses ÷ total sales

Operating expenses as a percentage of sales. Testing expenses excluded from numerator.

Profit growthvs previous period

Net profit change vs the preceding period of equal length. Shown only when both periods have complete buying prices.

Best sales dayHighest daily sales ₹

Calendar day with the highest combined fuel + lube sales in the range.

Worst profit dayLowest daily profit

Day with the lowest daily profit (sales − fuel cost − expenses). Used to spot cost or expense spikes.

Days with salesActive trading days

Count of calendar days where DSR recorded sales > ₹0, shown as “X of Y calendar days”.

Days profitable / Loss daysDaily profit sign

Days where daily profit (chart series) is positive vs negative. Based on daily fuel margin and expenses; may differ from period net profit if buying prices were incomplete on some days.

Charts & insights Visual analytics and automated takeaways
Sales trendDaily net sale (₹)

Line chart of daily fuel net sale + lube per day across the range.

Revenue by fuelStacked petrol vs diesel (₹)

Daily fuel revenue split by product (net litres × selling rate).

Profit & expensesDaily bars

Daily profit (green) vs expenses (red). Daily profit = sales − fuel cost − expenses for that date.

Fuel mix (volume)Petrol vs diesel (L)

Doughnut chart of total net litres by product for the period.

InsightsAutomated summary cards

Generated from the metrics above — best/worst days, margins, volume mix, expense ratio alerts, and reconciliation notes (e.g. missing buying prices, lube included).
