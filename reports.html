Trading account Stock-based debit and credit layout

A traditional trading account listing what came in against what went out for the selected period. Debit and credit totals must match.

Layout

Credit
Sales (petrol + diesel + lube/billing) + Closing stock at cost
Debit
Opening stock at cost + Purchases (fuel receipts) + Gross income c/d
  • Fuel sales: net litres (meter minus testing) × selling rate.
  • Stock valued at landed buying rate from receipt days (pre-VAT ₹/KL + VAT + delivery).
  • Lube/billing: invoice totals in the period.
Gross income Balancing figure · trading account only

Gross income appears only on the trading account as “Gross income c/d” on the debit side. It is the amount that balances debit and credit — not the same as net profit.

Formula Gross income = Sales + Closing stock (at cost) − Opening stock (at cost) − Purchases

Trading result from stock movement and sales, before operating expenses (rent, salary, electricity, etc.). Answers “how did trading perform?” — not “what is cash profit after all bills?”

Profit & loss (net profit) Margin-based · Dashboard & Analysis

Margin-based P&L for the period. Identical calculation on Dashboard → P&L, Analysis, and this report when the date range and buying prices match.

Formula Fuel gross profit = net litres × (selling rate − landed buying rate)
Gross profit = fuel gross profit + lube/billing sales
Net profit = gross profit − operating expenses
Included
Fuel margin, lube/billing invoices, logged operating expenses.
Excluded
MS/HS testing and density test expenses (handled in day closing).
Requires
Pre-VAT ₹/KL on every receipt day in range — otherwise net profit shows “—”.
Comparing figures Gross income vs gross profit vs net profit
Term Source Meaning
Gross income Trading account Stock-based trading result; opening/closing dip stock at cost.
Gross profit P&L · Dashboard · Analysis Per-litre fuel margin plus lube/billing; no stock adjustment.
Net profit P&L · Dashboard · Analysis Gross profit minus operating expenses (testing excluded).

Gross income ≠ net profit for the same period — expected. Gross income ignores operating expenses; net profit subtracts them. P&L net profit should match across Dashboard, Analysis, and Reports when dates and buying prices align.

Dashboard day summary Day-closing cash view · not P&L

The Dashboard Day summary is a single-date cash reconciliation: total sale ₹ includes testing litres; expenses include all categories. It supports day closing and in-hand cash — not margin-based net profit. Use Dashboard P&L, Analysis, or Reports P&L for profit calculations.
